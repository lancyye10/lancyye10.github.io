Moon travel!

About the moon

The moon is very different from Earth, it has no wind, weather or life, and is boiling hot by day and freezing at night. There are lots of saucer shaped holes called craters.

Interesting facts

  • The moon does not give off it's own light. It is visible from Earth because it shares the Sun's light.
  • The Moon takes about 27.3 days to spin around once, and around the Earth too. That's why we see only one side of the Moon.
  • People visited the Moon six times already.

Lunar Eclipse

Sometimes when we look at the night sky, the Moon was visible now, but then it gets smaller and smaller and it sometimes becomes red! That is called a lunar eclipse. When there is a total lunar eclipse, the Earth's shadow, with the Sun's light, falls on the Moon so that it looked like it's red. This is called a blood moon.

About

  1. Only very less people on Earth could see a lunar eclipse once.
  2. Sometimes you see a total lunar eclipse, other times you only see a part of the eclipse.
  3. A blood moon is visible in a total lunar eclipse.

Moon visit

The Moon is fun to visit. You could jump much higher there, and there's way less gravity. You could collect moon rocks there, too. Be careful with the craters!
